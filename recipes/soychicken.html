Soy-Marinated Chicken Breast

Description

The chicken breast turns out succulent and flavourful after being marinated for 24 hours. The marinade is then turned into a delicious sauce that can be enjoyed with the chicken or another dish at a later time. Best of all, this dish is incredibly simple to make, requiring only a little planning ahead.

Ingredients

  • 4 Chicken Breasts, deskinned
  • 1/2 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1/4 cup Sake
  • 1/4 cup Water
  • 3 tbsp Cane Sugar
  • 3 cloves Garlic, peeled
  • 3cm piece Ginger, sliced

Steps

  1. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag.
  2. Combine the rest of the ingredients and mix well to ensure that the sugar is dissolved.
  3. Pour the marinade into the bag with the chicken breasts, making sure that it gets everywhere.
  4. Seal the bag with as little air inside as possible and place in the fridge to marinade over night, for approximately 24 hours.
  5. Pre-heat the oven to 200 degrees celsius.
  6. Take the chicken breasts out of the marinade dripping off most of the excess liquid and place them on a parchment paper lined baking sheet.
  7. Bake in the oven for approximately 20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  8. Meanwhile, strain the solids out of the marinade and place in saucepan.
  9. Bring the sauce to a simmer and reduce the liquid to a good taste and consistency.
  10. The sauce needs to be strained one more time as the "scum" from the chicken breasts solidifies.
  11. Let the chicken rest for at least 5 minutes after removing from the oven.
  12. Serve topped with some of the sauce and optionally thinly sliced spring onions and sesame seeds.
